# Recovery-Outcome Evidence for Sensor / Biofeedback Gait Rehabilitation

Paper list assembled to fill the gap in **Resch et al. (2025)**, *"Smart Walking Aids with
Sensor Technology for Gait Support and Health Monitoring: A Scoping Review"* (Technologies 13, 346).
That scoping review mapped instrumented canes/walkers/crutches but reported **only
measurement/classification accuracy — no clinical recovery outcomes** (just one 10-week study,
no controlled trials).

Sources below were found via Semantic Scholar (`s2cli`). Semantic Scholar `paperId` included for each.

## Honest framing (read before citing)

- **No RCT exists for instrumented canes/walkers/crutches specifically** with recovery outcomes —
  Resch et al. were correct on that narrow point. The evidence below is from the **adjacent**
  modality: sensor/biofeedback gait training and load-sensing insoles. Cite it as such, not as
  evidence about smart canes themselves.
- The strongest, most defensible claims are the **meta-analyses (Group B)**.
- Several individual RCTs are small (n = 24–32) or pilots.
- Abstracts for Carpinella 2017, Gordt 2017, and Raaben 2018 were publisher-elided in Semantic
  Scholar — **verify their effect sizes from full text before quoting numbers**.

## Quick-pick guidance

- **Need one headline claim that sensor biofeedback aids recovery** → Wang et al. 2025 (#8).
- **Need the closest analogue to a smart walking aid reducing recovery time** → Castellarin 2022 (#4) load-feedback insole, TKA fast-track discharge.
- **Need a balanced/honest counterpoint** → Mao 2024 (#9, wearables underperformed) and Ambrosini 2020 (#6, no overall superiority).
