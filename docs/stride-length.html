How Moon Walk calculates stride length

Cane Mode · single shaft-mounted IMU (gyroscope + accelerometer)

From a swinging cane to a stride length, step by step.

Distance Estimator · Tier-2 trend metric

Your cane plants its tip, rotates about that tip like an inverted pendulum, then lifts and swings to the next plant. One plant-to-plant period is a Stick Cycle. The horizontal distance the cane travels in that cycle is the stride length. Here are the two ways to compute it — the simple geometry, and the validated one we actually ship.

1The geometry

The IMU sits on the shaft a fixed distance L above the tip — your one calibration constant ("enter your stick length"). As you step, the cane sweeps through a swing angle θ measured from vertical. Drag the sliders:

stride ≈ 2·L·sin θ
0.85 m

2Version A — the simple geometry

If the tip is a perfect fixed pivot, the IMU (and your hand) swing forward by L·sinθ. Plant the cane ahead at angle θ, push past vertical to θ on the other side, and the ground covered is the sum:

stride ≈ L · sin(θfwd) + L · sin(θback) simple pendulum geometry

You get θ by integrating the gyroscope over the swing, resetting to zero at each plant:

θ(t) = ∫ ω(t) dt   (θ = 0 at each plant) gyro integration

Cheap, and roughly what the architecture doc describes. But it assumes a perfect fixed pivot and ignores that your arm also translates — so it's a coarse Tier-2 approximation. Fine for a first cut.

3Version B — the validated way ship this

Dang & Suh (2018) measured a cane-mounted IMU to 0.67–1.27 % distance error with this approach. Instead of trusting the geometry, it computes the IMU's actual velocity from the gyro, then integrates — once per Stick Cycle, between plant n and plant n+1:

  1. Detect the plant (ZUPT anchor). Both true at once (Brahms 2018 thresholds): |ω| < 3.5 rad/s AND 0.3g < |a| < 1.7g. At a plant the tip velocity is ≈ 0 — this is what kills drift. Without a force sensor, this stillness test is your plant detector.
  2. Track cane tilt with a Madgwick filter (gyro + accel, no magnetometer). Gives orientation C(t) at ~0.9° error (Gorordo Fernandez 2020).
  3. Velocity from the gyro — not the accelerometer. While the tip is planted the cane rotates rigidly about it, so the IMU's velocity is purely rotational. This is the key move: no double-integrating noisy acceleration.
    vworld(t) = C(t) · ( ω(t) × rtip )    ⇒ |v| ≈ L·ω inverted-pendulum velocity · Dang & Suh 2018
    rtip is the vector from IMU to tip, magnitude L.
  4. Integrate velocity over the stride to get horizontal displacement:
    Δx = ∫tₙtₙ₊₁ vhorizontal(t) dt trapezoidal
  5. Drift correction (Sijobert 2015). At the next plant velocity should return to ≈ 0; any leftover is accumulated drift — subtract it as a linear ramp:
    vcorr(t) = v(t) − [ v(tₙ₊₁) / (tₙ₊₁ − tₙ) ] · (t − tₙ) linear de-drift
  6. Stride length = the corrected horizontal displacement between consecutive plants. Then velocity = stride × cadence for the dashboard.

What it looks like in hub.py

Today the hub only finds cadence (gyro threshold crossings). Per detected cycle you'd add:

theta = integrate(gyro_swing, dt) # gyro arc v = L * gyro_swing # |v| = L·ω (pendulum velocity) disp = trapz(v_horizontal, dt) # integrate over the stride disp -= linear_drift_ramp(v_at_plant) # de-drift to the next plant stride = disp velocity = stride * cadence # feeds the dashboard

4The honest caveats

⚠ Report this as a trend, never a clinical absolute

  • IMU-only today. Plant detection is IMU-stillness, not measured force — a light touch can look like a weight-bearing plant. The dual threshold (Step 1) is the best defence until the FSR arrives.
  • L must be calibrated per user. Error in L scales straight into stride error — that's why the app asks for stick length at setup.
  • Expect meaningful absolute error from a single-IMU stride estimate. Calibrate against the User's own baseline; never show clinical centimetres. This is ADR-0001 / ADR-0005 — wellness, not medicine.
  • Dragging breaks it. No plant ⇒ no ZUPT ⇒ runaway drift (Dang & Suh flag this explicitly).

A signal you may prefer over stride length

Stride length is the hard, inaccurate metric. Phinyomark (2025) shows stride-interval variability — coefficient of variation CV = (σ/μ)·100% and the detrended-fluctuation exponent αDFA — is more sensitive to gait change (Cohen's d up to 2.87) and needs only the plant timestamps you already detect: no integration, no L, no drift. It may be the smarter headline number for the Drift / Alert pipeline, with stride length kept as a secondary trend.
